About me

I received the M.ED degree in language and literacy from Harvard University. I am interested in how home and school practices shape children's early language and literacy development. My current research explores 1) how teachers’ shared-book reading strategies influence children's vocabulary development in rural China preschools; 2) the development of research-based tools to evaluate classroom interaction in underserved areas. I am also co-founder of Button Baby, an educational startup that aims at bringing simple and effective interactions to families.

Research interest: early language and literacy, bilingual education, parenting, educational media

One Village One Preschool (OVOP) Project
OVOP is a government-supported public intervention launched by the China Development Research Foundation in collaboration with Professor Catherine E. Snow, which provided disadvantaged rural and minority children in central and western rural China with access to early childhood education (ECE). OVOP has established about 2300 centers in ten provinces, enrolling over 170,000 3–6-year olds, free of charge for all. This project is one of the World Innovation Summit for Education (WISE) Awards winners.

  • Carried out an independent study on the relationship between shared book reading strategies & children’s language development, transcribed & coded video according to self-developed coding scheme; analyzed & visualized data by Stata

  • Conducted literature review of the academic & social outcomes of early childhood education, topics include picture book reading strategies, classroom organization, child development of vocabulary & social-emotional skills, the use of iPad & other technologies

  • Designed researched-based teaching plans & classroom activities for 80+ OVOP classrooms in Yunnan & Sichuan Provinces, co-authored handbooks for teachers & students during the pandemic
